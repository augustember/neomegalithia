Abilities

Abilities are a special action. Most abilities require a dice roll, but not all. Some have specific targets for their effects and some do not and some have an energy cost and some don't.

Ability Roll

When something would have you make an ability roll, you roll 3d6 + [the ability's Core Bonus] + [Advantage Bonus] vs a target’s Threshold.

Targets

The concept of "targets" refers to the focus of an action, whether it's an attack, a spell, a skill attempt, or any other directed effort within the game. Targets can be enemy combatants, allies, objects, or even specific locations within the environment.

Types of Targets:

  • Single Target: Actions aimed at a specific individual or object. Precision is key, and effects are concentrated on one entity.
  • Multiple Targets: Some abilities allow for affecting multiple entities within a specified range or area. These are ideal for handling groups or strategically influencing the battlefield.
  • Area of Effect (AoE): Targets within a designated area, often used for spells or area-based attacks. The effect applies uniformly within this zone, impacting all within its bounds.
  • Self: Some abilities or effects target the user, enhancing their abilities or providing protection.

Other Stuff:

  • Line of Sight: For most actions, a clear line of sight to the target is necessary. Obstructions can prevent targeting or affect the effectiveness of an action.
  • Range: Different actions have varying ranges. Players must be within the effective range to target successfully.

    Time Limits of Effects

    When an ability says something like “for the next minute X happens” or something like that, what it means is that the effect happens for the next ten rounds, including the round that effect was initiated.

    Ability Stuff

    Damaging

    Abilities categorized under Damaging directly inflict harm upon foes. These can vary from physical strikes to magical attacks, all designed to reduce an enemy's health points (HP). Mastery in damaging abilities often leads to increased damage output or additional effects upon hitting the target.

    Attack

    The Attack trait denotes abilities that require a confrontation with a target, typically involving a roll against a one of a target’s Thresholds. These are combat abilities.

    Control

    Abilities with the Buff/Debuff trait either enhance an ally's capabilities (buffs) or impair an enemy's effectiveness (debuffs). These abilities can turn the tide of battle by boosting your party's Strength or weakening your adversaries.

    Support

    These are abilities that enhance a character, either with a temporary status boost, restoration of health, or some other effect which aids creatures in various ways. Weapon

    Weapon abilities are those that rely on a character's equipped weapon to execute. The effects and damage of these abilities are often determined by the weapon's attributes, allowing for diverse combat strategies based on equipment choice.

    Magical

    Magical abilities draw upon the ancient energies of Terra Megalithia, allowing characters to cast spells that can heal, harm, protect, or manipulate the environment. Mastery in magical abilities can unlock powerful effects and spell combinations.

    Utility

    Abilities categorized under Utility provide characters with non-combat advantages, aiding in navigation, exploration, problem-solving, and enhancing the party's ability to interact with the world around them. Mastery in utility abilities allows for creative solutions to obstacles, the gathering of critical information, and the avoidance of direct confrontations.

    Duration of Effects of Abilities

    Some effects of abilities refer to a duration such as "until end of Fast Times." This is fairly obvious during Fast Times. However, when these abilities are used outside during Good Times these abilities have a duration of 20 minutes. If 20 minutes of in-game time somehow passes during Fast Times, don't worry about it, the durations of those effects are still are still in effect somehow.

    When an ability has a duration for its effects and that ability is performed on the same target again, the duration for that ability simply resets to its initial duration.

    When Damage is a Result of a Single Roll Against Multiple Targets

    When an ability targets more than one target and wants a single roll against all targets, the damage of that ability is the same for all targets.
