Character Creation (As a Level 1)

1.Choose a Name

It can be whatever.

2. Get Your Starting Level

As a brand new character you start at level 1.

3. Get Your Starting Dice!

You start with a number of different dice types which you will be using to roll for your various skills and abilities.

You start with:

  • 2 d12s
  • 3 d10s
  • 4 d8s
  • 5 d6s

You might want to be care with how you assign these, since you are going to be assigning these across all of your skills, defense rolls, and abilities. You are absolutely capable of creating a very imbalanced character, such as one who hits hard with her abilities but is absolutely trash at performing skills, etc.

4. Assign Dice to Slots of Your Defense Rolls

Your Defense Rolls are what you use to roll against something that is acting against you such as an environmental hazard or attack. Each Defense Roll has 2 Dice Slots. Your starting dice which you assign to these slots are: 1 d10, 2 d8s, and 3 d6s. You may put 1 of each type of die in a slot of your choosing.

Your Defense Rolls are:

  • Toughness
  • Evasion
  • Willpower

5. Assign Dice to The Dice Slots of your Skills

Skills are a type of roll which you use to overcome various challenges.

Your Skills are:

  • Athletics
  • Insight
  • Knowledge
  • Performance
  • Search
  • Sense
  • Social
  • Search
  • Stealth

6. Starting Experience Points

Your experienced points allow you to gain levels when they meet certain thresholds. Perhaps unsurprisingly, your experience points start at 0 as a level 1.

7. Select a Playable Creature

Pick one creature from the Playable Creatures section. Each species has distinct traits and abilities. Choose one that fits your vision for your character's strengths, weaknesses, and cultural background. The playable creatures are: Faun, Minotaur, and Merfolk.

Your starting Item Slots, HP maximum, Size, and Speed are determined by which ability you choose. You gain all the benefits of your chosen species as described in their respective sections.

Fast Link: Playable Creatures

8. Get Some Actions

As a level 1 you get: 2 standard actions and 1 extra action.

9. Equip Your Character

Select your items and equipment from the starting equipment section. You have several slots which you may use to equip your various pieces of gear.

Choose one of the following to gain:
  • One big weapon.
  • One medium weapon and one shield.
  • One medium weapon and two small weapons.
  • Two medium weapons.

    • You gain the following:

  • One relic of your choice.
  • One torso armor of your choice.
  • Two field items of your choice.
  • Two single-use items of your choice.

    • You may decide to not take all of the items which you receive. After all, you may have only have up to 10 Small items of a single unique type in a single Item Slot and only 1 Big item in an Item Slot. Item slots may not share items of different unique types.

    9. Acquire Your Starting Ability Rank, Starting, Abilities

    You have an Ability Rank which starts at Novice. You may only take or use abilities which you have the necessary Ability Rank for.

    You receive all of the Default Abilities. You are granted all of the Default Abilities. Among the Default Abilities, you may assign 1 of them your Big Bonus, 1 your Medium Bonus, and the rest of the Default Abilities receive your Small Bonus.

    For non-Default Abilities, you may take 3 abilities for each Rank of ability you are qualified for (Rankless and Novice). For each of those 3 chosen abilities, assign your 3 Core Bonuses among them.

    11. Determine Your Damage Buffers

    Damage Buffers are numbers which represent how much damage you negate when you take damage (what some RPGs call soak). Natural Defense represents your innate ability to withstand damage whereas Armor Defense represents equipment which is allowing you to withstand damage. Certain species grant you a certain Natural Defense number and some armor you get from equipment grants you a number for your Armor Defense. Your Defense Total is the combination of Natural and Armor Defense Scores.

    For example, if you had a Natural Defense of 1 and you were wearing armor which granted you an Armor Defense of 2, then your Defense Total would be 3. And so when you take damage, you would subtract that damage amount from this Defense Total.
