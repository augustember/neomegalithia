Character Creation (As a Level 1)

1.Choose a Name

It can be whatever.

2. Get Your Starting Level

As a brand new character you start at level 1.

3. Get Your Core Bonuses

Your Core Bonuses are the main numbers that add on to your dice rolls for skills and abilities. As a level 1, your Big, Medium, and Small core bonuses are +3, +2, and +1 respectively.

These bonuses are assigned to your abilities and skills.

4. Determine Skill Bonuses and Thresholds

Your Main Skills are Strength, Athletics, and Wisdom. Assign your Big, Medium and Small bonuses among these 3 skills.

Your expertise skills are Charm, Detection, Inspect, Medicine, Knowledge, Stealth, and Survival.

Select 2 skills to receive your Big Core Bonus, another 2 skills to receive your Medium Bonus, and 2 additional skills for your Small Bonus. Any remaining skills start with a bonus of +0 and threshold of 10.

All of your skills have numbers called thresholds which serve as target numbers for various things to roll against. A threshold is determined by 10 plus whichever Core Bonus a skill has been assigned to it plus whatever gear bonus a particular piece of equipment may be granting for that threshold. In other words:

The Threshold of all skills is determined with the formula 10 + [Core Bonus Value] + [Gear Increase] + [Trait Increase] .

5. Starting Experience Points

Your experienced points allow you to gain levels when they meet certain thresholds. Perhaps unsurprisingly, your experience points start at 0 as a level 1.

6. Select a Playable Creature

Pick one creature from the Playable Creatures section. Each species has distinct traits and abilities. Choose one that fits your vision for your character's strengths, weaknesses, and cultural background. The playable creatures are: Faun, Minotaur, and Merfolk.

Your starting Encumbrance Capacity, HP maximum, Energy Maximum, Size, and Speed are determined by which ability you choose. You gain all the benefits of your chosen species as described in their respective sections.

Playable Creatures

7. Get Some Actions

As a level 1 you get: 2 standard actions and 1 extra action.

8. Equip Your Character

Select your items and equipment from the starting equipment section. You have several slots which you may use to equip your various pieces of gear.

As a level 1 character you receive:
  • 2 Weapons of your choice from the weapons section
  • 1 Piece of armor of your choice from the armor section + 1 shield
  • 1 of each of all the field items in the field items section.
  • 2 single-use items from the Single-Use Items. You gain 5 of each of whichever Single-Use items you chose. You may decide to not take all of the items which you receive. After all, you may have only have up to 10 Small items of a single unique type in a single Item Slot and only 1 Big item in an Item Slot. Item slots may not share items of different unique types.

    9. Acquire Your Starting Ability Rank, Starting, Abilities

    You have an Ability Rank which starts at Novice. You may only take or use abilities which you have the necessary Ability Rank for.

    You receive all of the Default Abilities. You are granted all of the Default Abilities. Among the Default Abilities, you may assign 1 of them your Big Bonus, 1 your Medium Bonus, and the rest of the Default Abilities receive your Small Bonus.

    For non-Default Abilities, you may take 3 abilities for each Rank of ability you are qualified for (Rankless and Novice). For each of those 3 chosen abilities, assign your 3 Core Bonuses among them.

    11. Determine Your Defense Scores

    Defense scores are numbers which represent how much damage you negate when you take damage (what some RPGs call soak). Natural Defense represents your innate ability to withstand damage whereas Armor Defense represents equipment which is allowing you to withstand damage. Certain species grant you a certain Natural Defense number and some armor you get from equipment grants you a number for your Armor Defense. Your Defense Total is the combination of Natural and Armor Defense Scores.

    For example, if you had a Natural Defense of 1 and you were wearing armor which granted you an Armor Defense of 2, then your Defense Total would be 3. And so when you take damage, you would subtract that damage amount from this Defense Total.
