Gear

Pieces of gear grant certain effects. Weapons, relics, and armor are all considered gear. Gear must be equipped for their effects to happen.

Switching Equipment

If you need to equip something else during fast times, it costs 1 extra action.

Equipment and Equip Slots

Some items may be equipped. The effects of those items only happen when they are equipped. However, you have limited slots for a particular type of equipable item. Those slots are:

  • Head slot
  • 2 hand slots
  • 1 shoes slot
  • 1 armor slot
  • 2 relic slots
  • 1 glove slot
  • 1 strap slot

Encumbrance

Items all have an encumbrance value. You have an encumbrance capacity which is determined by your species and sometimes the effects of gear you are wearing. If your items would cause you to have a greater encumbrance than you have encumbrance capacity for, you take a penalty on all ability rolls, any skill check using Agility, and your movement speed is reduced by half.

Switching Equipment

If you need to equip something else during fast times, it costs 1 extra action per piece of equipment being switched out.

Starting Equipment Options

You gain:

  • 2 Weapons of your choice from the weapons section
  • 1 Piece of armor of your choice from the armor section + 1 shield
  • 1 of each of all the field items in the field items section
  • 1 of each of all the single-use items from the single-use section.

Weapons

Oddly enough, weapons are special tools used to attack. Weapons are used in tandem with various attack abilities.

Weapon Damage

Weapons, like abilities, have half and full damage values. The damage a weapon deals corresponds to the roll for an ability that used that weapon.

Weapon Traits

Weapons have unique traits, yada yada.

Melee

This weapon hits its targets up close (5 feet or less, with exceptions). They are compatible with Melee abilities unless otherwise specified.

Ranged

This weapon hits its targets at a long distance.

Thrown

These weapons can be thrown 50 feet.

Base Damage

This is a static number of a weapon or ability which is added on to the damage of an attack roll.

Light

Can be thrown 20 feet. These can be used with either Strength or Agility for their roll.

Heavy

Can be thrown 10 feet. They also must use your Strength to roll for them.

Small

Weapons with this trait are wielded in one hand, allowing for versatility in combat. Characters can pair a one-handed weapon with a shield, another weapon, or keep a hand free for casting spells or using items.

Big

These weapons require both hands to wield because of their size or weight but typically offer greater damage or special effects. Using a two-handed weapon means you cannot use shields or other items simultaneously in combat.

Versatile

Can be wielded either one or two-handed. Its damage increases when wielded with two.

Range: X

Weapons with a range trait can hit targets from a distance, with "X" specifying the maximum range in feet. Ranged weapons allow characters to engage foes without entering melee combat, ideal for tactical positioning.

Strength-based

The effectiveness of these weapons depends on the wielder's Strength stat. They benefit from higher damage modifiers tied to Strength, making them suitable for physically powerful characters.

Wisdom-Based

Some weapons draw on the wielder's Wisdom stat, blending martial prowess with magical or mental intensity. These weapons often have special effects beyond physical damage.

Damage

This trait indicates the base damage a weapon deals On a hit,. Damage can be enhanced through upgrades, abilities, and exploding rolls.

Ability Compatibility

Attacking with a weapon requires that you have a category of move compatible with it.

Recall

When this weapon is thrown, it comes back to you to be used again as long as your roll was successful. Weapons without this ability need to be physically retrieved once thrown somewhere.
