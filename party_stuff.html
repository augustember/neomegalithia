Party and Travel Stuff

You exist within a party of other playable characters. Collectively, you and your other party members have supplies, renown, and other things which you all share as a party.

Wagon

Your party has a wagon, which is a vehicle that your party uses to go from place to place quickly. This is what stores your supply items. This wagon also has its own separate stats and abilities.

Each vehicle has different gas usage per day, so pay attention to that

Currency

The various factions have their forms of currency which are used to trade.

  • The Ecosynths use barter only
  • The Successors use Successor Dollars
  • The Gaia Nation use precious stones such as quartz, rubies, and sapphires. The value of the stones often change from tribe-to-tribe.
  • Disciples of the Weaver trade in owed favors which may be pawned off on others.

Supplies

Supplies are items that are shared across your party and are stored in the party's wagon. Each wagon has its own benefits, features, and item slots. Certain items such as rations, types of parts for trading or crafting, and different types of treasure are stored here.

Rations and Hunger

On the road, you need to eat in order to avoid hunger. Hunger is a stat your entire party has, which may affect you in various ways.

For every travel day, you use up 1 ration, unless you decide not to eat that day. If you don't eat at least 1 ration a day, you acquire 1 Hunger Damage. Hunger Damage does not propogate or be shaken off, and cannot be healed with a skill or ability. You lose all acquired Hunger Damage by eating a ration.

Dates and Travel Days

It may go without saying, but every in-game day has its own date. This is particularly important for supply usage, such as using rations and fuel over a certain amount of travel time. Years in Neomegalithia have 360 days (or 361 days on leap years). There are four months in a year (Spring, Summer, Autumn, and Winter) which have 90 days each. Nobody bothers with named days of the week, instead opting for the date before the year, such as Winter 25th. A full date might look like "Winter 25th, 3150."

This may sound like a drag to hear, but your party actually does keep track of the date from day-to-day.

Renown

This is how famous your party is, and also what your party is famous for. Your renown will have a number of descriptive tags such as (but not limited to): helpfulness, selflessness, brave, cowardly, greedy, murderous, destructive, etc. Your Renown also has a number, which determines if certain characters have or have not heard of your party.

Check Points

Every so often while traveling your party will run into noteworthy situations and be made to make a decision which lead to various consequences, either good or bad.
